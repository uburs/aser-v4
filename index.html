Study 2

The purpose of Study 2 is for you to interact with four different caption prototypes, answer few quiz based on the captions, and do the post-study surveys.

Prototype 1: Basic Real-Time Captioning
The first page of the prototype demonstrates a straightforward implementation of real-time captioning. The captions are displayed in a dedicated caption box adjacent to the video feed of the speaker. This setup allows users to read the captions while still being able to observe the speaker’s facial expressions and body language. The text is animated to appear sequentially, simulating a live transcription environment.

Prototype 2: Real-time Caption Overlay on Video Feed
This prototype introduces a real-time caption overlay on the video feed of the speaker. This ensures that users can read captions while simultaneously accessing the speaker’s facial expressions.

Prototype 3: Enhanced Captioning with Emphasis
The third page focuses on integrating emotional expressions into the captions. Emotional words are identified and highlighted using color coding to reflect the speaker’s tone (e.g., green for happy, blue for sad). The captions are overlaid on the live video feed, and the text moves upward, similar to real-time scrolling captions seen in live broadcasts.

Prototype 4: Real-time Caption with Emoji and Visualization
The fourth page explores a more interactive approach by introducing emoji that changes facial expressions and gestures based on the emotional tone of the text.
